# Goals & Patterns

The standing context the whole loop measures against. Loaded at the start of every
ritual. Keep it short and keep it current — this is Gate 0's output.

## Goals

The small set of outcomes worth your scarce attention. State each as an **outcome**
(a thing that changes in the world), not an **activity** (a thing you do). Keep the
list short enough that "the one thing today" is a real choice. Delete a goal the
moment it stops earning its slot.

- **[Goal 1]** — the outcome. _How you'd know it moved:_ [observable signal].
- **[Goal 2]** — the outcome. _How you'd know it moved:_ [observable signal].
- **[Goal 3]** — the outcome. _How you'd know it moved:_ [observable signal].

> Example (delete):
> - **Ship product improvements customers actually notice.** _How you'd know:_ a
>   customer mentions the change unprompted, or the metric it targets moves.
> - **Write in the margins.** _How you'd know:_ one published piece a month.

## Named avoidance patterns

The traps you already know you fall into. The morning and weekly gates check your
focus and your misses against these by name — naming them here is what lets the
system catch you. Add to this list from `/weekly-signals` over time.

| Pattern | What it looks like |
|---------|--------------------|
| Infrastructure over output | Building tools/systems instead of shipping the deliverable |
| Research loops without shipping | Deep dives that never produce an artifact |
| Starting new before finishing | New project while the current one is unfinished |
| Skipping the weekly review | Which breaks the pattern detection the loop runs on |

_(Replace or extend with your own. The four above are common; keep the ones that
are true for you.)_
